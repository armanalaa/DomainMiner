 # DellStore2 — Business Workflows

## 1. Customer Registration

Performed By: Customer Service Representative (CSR)

Activity:
- CSR enters customer details such as name, email, and address into the `CUSTOMER` table.
- CSR assigns a unique customer ID to the new account.
- CSR sets up user credentials for the customer in the `CUSTOMER` table.

## 2. Account Management

Performed By: Customer Service Representative (CSR)

Activity:
- CSR retrieves customer details from the `CUSTOMER` table based on the customer ID.
- CSR updates customer information in the `CUSTOMER` table when necessary.
- CSR resets customer passwords as requested by customers.

## 3. Product Browsing

Performed By: Customers, Sales Representatives

Activity:
- Customers browse products by actor using the `public.browse_by_actor` function.
- Customers browse products by category using the `public.browse_by_category` function.
- Customers browse products by title using the `public.browse_by_title` function.

## 4. Order Placement

Performed By: Customers, Sales Representatives

Activity:
- Customers add desired products to their shopping carts.
- Customers proceed to checkout and confirm their orders.
- Sales representatives place orders on behalf of customers.

## 5. Inventory Management

Performed By: Warehouse Staff, Purchasing Teams

Activity:
- Warehouse staff update the `inventory` table with current product quantities.
- Purchasing teams order new stock for low inventory items from suppliers.
- Warehouse staff receive and record deliveries in the `inventory` table.

## 6. Order Fulfillment

Performed By: Warehouse Staff, Shipping Teams

Activity:
- Warehouse staff pick and pack ordered products for shipping.
- Shipping teams generate shipping labels and prepare packages for delivery.
- Shipping teams update the `inventory` table with the new product quantities.

## 7. Reordering Management

Performed By: Inventory Managers, Purchasing Teams

Activity:
- Inventory managers monitor low stock levels in the `reorder` table.
- Purchasing teams issue purchase orders for reorders based on the information in the `reorder` table.
- Inventory managers update the `inventory` and `reorder` tables with new product quantities once reordered items are received.
