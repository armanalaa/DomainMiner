 # TPC-H — Business Workflows

## 1. Customer Management

Performed By: Customer Service Representatives, Sales Managers

Activity:
- Customer service representatives register new customers by recording their names, addresses, nationalities, phone numbers, account balances, market segments, and comments.
- Sales managers update customer information as needed, including changes to account balances and marketing segments.

## 2. Order Processing

Performed By: Sales Representatives, Clerks

Activity:
- Sales representatives create new orders by associating them with existing customers, setting order statuses, total prices, order dates, order priorities, and comments.
- Clerks process incoming orders by assigning ship priorities and recording the relevant details in the orders table.

## 3. Product Catalog Management

Performed By: Product Managers, Warehouse Staff

Activity:
- Product managers register new items in the catalog by recording product codes, names, categories, list prices, and comments.
- Warehouse staff update product information as needed, including changes to retail prices and comments.

## 4. Supply Chain Management

Performed By: Purchasing Teams, Warehouse Staff

Activity:
- Purchasing teams issue purchase orders by associating them with specific suppliers, recording part keys, available quantities, supply costs, and comments.
- Warehouse staff track inventory levels by updating the availability of parts in the partsupp table.

## 5. Nation and Region Management

Performed By: Geographical Information Specialists

Activity:
- Geographical information specialists manage geographical data by recording nation names, regions, and comments in the nation and region tables.

## 6. Supplier Management

Performed By: Procurement Managers

Activity:
- Procurement managers register new suppliers by recording supplier names, addresses, nationalities, phone numbers, account balances, and comments.

## 7. Reporting and Analysis

Performed By: Data Analysts, Management

Activity:
- Data analysts generate reports based on the data stored in the various tables, providing insights into sales, inventory levels, customer behavior, and more.
- Management uses these reports to make informed decisions about business strategy and operations.
